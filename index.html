Niki Arrogante - Profile

Hello! My name is Niki Arrogante, student number: s3851498. My email address is s3851498@student.rmit.edu.au.

I was born and raised in Australia after my parents migrated from the Philippines around thirty years ago. I completed my high school education in 2006 then immediately entered the workforce to help support my family. During my school years I was a high performer, and an active member of the student representative council as a vice captain. Unfortunately, my father passed away early in his life during my high school years, so I put my chance to go to university aside to keep the household afloat.

In my spare time I enjoy a range of hobbies, but my passions are with music, motorbikes, and gaming. At one point I was hoping to become a musician-producer, but life has a habit of carrying you in other directions. Instead, I floated through some odd jobs here and there aimlessly for a few years trying to work out what I wanted in life. After having my wonderful daughter, I realised that I needed to rekindle my passion for learning and go back to school.

After discussing it with my partner, I left my steady 9-to-5 job and enrolled to study a Bachelor's in Information Technology, with the intention of swapping our household roles so that I could focus on studying, and take over once I've gained the skills I need to become a successful professional in the IT field.

As a youth, my father was very involved in keeping me up to date with rising technologies. As such, my childhood was spent with electronic games, computer parts, and old Microsoft OS builds (hello Windows NT!). My love for all technology only grew, but I never really considered it as an interest in IT specifically. Late last year I had finally brought my life to a place where going back to study was financially plausible, but I hadn't decided exactly where I would go. Around this time, I bought myself an Oculus Rift S, a final splurge before I cut off my steady income. From the moment I had used it, I felt a nostalgic rush that reminded me of the fascination of playing video games as a child. I realised then a career in IT would be a good place for me, where I could try to bring this feeling to other people too.

RMIT was recommended to me when I made my call to Open Universities. My advisor advocated their reputation and reviews seemed to be generally favourable. So RMIT ended up being my first choice.

My goals are to learn more about programming and the industry, in hopes of working in software development. Specifically, I hope to develop virtual reality programs in the future and be part of the movement in making VR a mainstream consumer product.
